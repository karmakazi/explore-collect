How It Works

Three Steps to Community Engagement

No app downloads. No accounts. No personal data collected. Visitors simply scan, collect, and win.

1

Scan a QR Code

Visitors find QR codes at participating locations — storefronts, cultural sites, parks, or event venues. A quick scan opens the web app instantly in their browser.

2

Collect Virtual Stamps

Each scan earns a virtual stamp on their digital passport. Visitors are motivated to explore more locations, discovering new businesses and attractions along the way.

3

Enter & Win

Once they’ve collected enough stamps, visitors can enter a contest or unlock rewards. It’s a fun incentive that keeps people engaged and coming back.

Why Discvr

Built for the Realities of the Public Sector

We designed this platform alongside a municipality, solving the real-world blockers that often prevent innovative community programs from getting off the ground.

Privacy-First Architecture

No names, emails, or phone numbers are ever collected. We use only the first three characters of a visitor’s postal code — enough for regional analytics, not enough to identify anyone. Built to meet the strictest municipal privacy requirements.

No App Download Required

It’s a progressive web app that runs entirely in the browser. Visitors scan and go — no App Store, no Play Store, no friction. Works on any smartphone.

Launch-Ready in Days

No lengthy RFP cycles or custom development timelines. We configure the platform for your community and hand you print-ready QR codes. You can be live in under a week.

Fully Branded Experience

Your logo, your colors, your messaging. The platform is white-labeled to feel like a natural extension of your existing community identity and programs.

Real-Time Analytics

See which locations are most visited, peak activity times, and regional visitor distribution — all without compromising anyone’s privacy. Export-ready reports for council presentations.

Enterprise-Grade Security

Hosted on secure, SOC 2-compliant infrastructure. Data encryption in transit and at rest. No third-party tracking scripts. Passes municipal IT security reviews with ease.

Live Case Study

Richmond Hill Passport to Culture

Our flagship deployment with the City of Richmond Hill brings arts, heritage, and cultural venues to life through an interactive passport experience.

The Challenge

Richmond Hill wanted to increase engagement with local cultural venues and events but needed a solution that met strict municipal privacy requirements, worked without app downloads, and could launch quickly alongside existing tourism campaigns.

The Solution

We deployed Discvr as a fully branded "Passport to Culture" experience. QR codes placed at cultural locations across the city let visitors collect stamps and enter to win prizes — all without sharing personal information.

The Result

A seamless launch that passed municipal IT and privacy reviews, funded in part by Central Counties Tourism. Residents and visitors are actively exploring cultural sites across the city.
